Introduction¶

Dataset Description:¶

This dataset describes the Data of patients in a brasillian clinic. Variables include Patient ID, Appointment ID, name, gender and age. It also includes data about their conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, alcoholism and handicaps. It also includes data for whether they showed up for their appointment or not and whether they recieved an sms to confirm the appointment or not.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

In this analysis two main questions are answered:

1-What are the variables the affect the patients tendency to show up to the appointment?

2-What are the main prevelant conditions based on a patients age?

Data Cleaning¶

The following procedures will be done for the data:

Appointment Day and schedueled date are objects, they will be converted to datetime to make it easier to handle.

There are no NaN data so no need to drop any rows or replace NaN values

Patient ID will be dropped to make correlations easier.

Duplicates were also checked and there were not any

Age has a value of -1 and 115 which were not logical and was removed

Handicap will be combined to be more clear in analysis

Spelling mistakes in columns such as handcap and hipertension will be corrected

Data shows the patient as No-show (yes) and show (no), it will be reversed and converted to intiger for easier handling

Handicap will be combined as either have handicap (1) and no handicap (0) for easier handling

Conclusions¶

2 Questions were answered:¶

1) what are the variables affecting patients tendency to show up to their appointments?¶

In summary,approximately 80% of the patients show up to their appointment regardless of their gender, scholarship or condition. Also recieveing SMS did not increase the show up percentage.

2) Are disease conditions affected by patient's age?¶

By examining the mean ages of patients for each condition, we can infere that the mean age for patients with hypertension is 61 years, diabetes is also 61 years, handicap is 52 years and alcoholism is 49 years.

Also by examining the relationship between age and different conditions, it is clear that as age increases, diabetes and hypertension prevalence increase, but age is not related to the presence of handicaps or alcoholism.

Limitations¶

This data is only based in one clinic, it could have been more representative if it was spread out on several clinics or for different countries., The data is also from 2016, it would have been more representative if it is more recent or covered a longer period of time.
